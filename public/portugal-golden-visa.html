Portugal Golden Visa 2026: Cost, Requirements & US Citizen Guide

For Americans wanting a European "plan B" — Schengen access, an EU residency, and a path to a second passport — Portugal's Golden Visa has been the most popular option in the world. US citizens are now the single largest group of applicants. But the program in 2026 looks very different from the one most online guides still describe. Here's what actually applies today.

What is the Portugal Golden Visa?

The Golden Visa is a residency-by-investment program. In exchange for a qualifying investment, you and your family receive Portuguese residency permits, the right to live, work and study in Portugal, and visa-free travel across the Schengen Area. Crucially, you are not required to relocate — the physical-presence requirement is only an average of about 7 days per year, making it a true backup plan rather than a forced move.

Why Americans choose it

  • EU access without moving: Schengen travel and an EU base, while you keep living in the US.
  • Family included: spouse, dependent children, and dependent parents can be added to one application.
  • Low stay requirement: ~7 days a year, far lighter than most residency programs.
  • Path to citizenship: an EU passport at the end of the road (timeline below).

Since the rules changed, the qualifying routes are capital-based — not property. The main options:

Why you can no longer buy property for it

This is the single biggest misconception. Since October 2023, real-estate investment no longer qualifies for the Portugal Golden Visa. The government removed the property route to ease housing-affordability pressure on locals. Any 2026 guide still telling you to "buy a €500K apartment for your Golden Visa" is out of date. If your goal is to own a home in Portugal, you still can — but that is a separate decision from the visa, and it won't earn you residency.

The new 10-year citizenship timeline

Historically, Golden Visa holders could apply for Portuguese citizenship after just 5 years — one of the program's biggest draws. Under Lei Orgânica 1/2026 (effective 19 May 2026), the general naturalization period for non-EU nationals — including Americans — was extended from 5 to 10 years, counted from the first residency card. Critical transitional rule: applications for citizenship submitted on or before 18 May 2026 are still governed by the prior 5-year rule under Lei 37/81. If your first residency card predates May 2026 and you are approaching the 5-year mark, confirm with your immigration lawyer whether you qualify under the transitional provision before the window closes.

US & Portuguese tax impact

Holding a Golden Visa does not, by itself, make you a Portuguese tax resident. You generally become tax resident only if you spend more than 183 days a year in Portugal or establish it as your habitual home. Because the program requires only ~7 days a year, most American holders never trigger Portuguese tax residency.

As a US citizen, however, you remain subject to US tax on worldwide income no matter where you live or invest. Your fund investment may also create US reporting obligations (for example, PFIC rules on certain foreign funds) — a critical, often-missed point worth reviewing with a cross-border CPA before you invest.

Step-by-step: applying as an American

  1. Get a Portuguese NIF (tax number) and open a Portuguese bank account.
  2. Choose your route — most pick a qualifying €500K fund.
  3. Run US tax due diligence on the specific fund (PFIC exposure) with a cross-border advisor.
  4. Make the investment and obtain proof of funds.
  5. Submit the Golden Visa application (typically via AIMA) with documents and biometrics.
  6. Receive your residency card, then renew per schedule while maintaining the investment.
  7. Maintain ~7 days/year presence and track your citizenship clock.

Is the Portugal Golden Visa still worth it in 2026?

For Americans who want EU optionality without relocating, it remains one of the strongest programs available — low stay requirement, family inclusion, Schengen access. But the value equation has shifted: the property route is gone, the entry cost is effectively €250K–€500K in capital, and the citizenship payoff now takes 10 years under Lei Orgânica 1/2026 (though a transitional 5-year window remains open for applications filed before 19 May 2026). Go in with current numbers, clean US tax planning, and a licensed advisor — not a 2022 blog post.

Frequently asked questions

Can Americans still get a Portugal Golden Visa in 2026?

Yes. US citizens are eligible and among the top applicants. The most common route is a €500,000 investment in a qualifying Portuguese fund. The real-estate option was removed in 2023.

How much do you need to invest?

The main routes are €500,000 in a qualifying fund, €500,000 in scientific research, or €250,000 in support of arts and cultural heritage. Buying real estate no longer qualifies.

Can you still buy property for the Golden Visa?

No. Since October 2023, real-estate investment no longer qualifies. Qualifying routes are funds, research, cultural donations, and job creation.

How long until citizenship?

Under Lei Orgânica 1/2026 (effective 19 May 2026), the naturalization timeline for Americans is 10 years from the first residency card. Transitional provision: citizenship applications filed on or before 18 May 2026 still qualify under the prior 5-year rule. Maintain ~7 days/year average presence to keep the clock running.

Do I pay Portuguese tax with a Golden Visa?

The visa alone does not make you tax resident; that generally requires 183+ days a year in Portugal. US citizens remain subject to US worldwide taxation regardless.
