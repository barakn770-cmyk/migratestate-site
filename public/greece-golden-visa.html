Greece Golden Visa 2026: Cost, Requirements & US Citizen Guide

MS By The MigrateState Editorial Team · Reviewed by a licensed international real-estate attorney  ·  Updated June 2026  ·  8 min read
Greece is one of the few Golden Visas still based on buying real estate. In 2026 the minimum is tiered: €800,000 in Athens, Thessaloniki, Mykonos, Santorini and larger islands; €400,000 in most other regions; and €250,000 only for restoration or commercial-to-residential conversions. It grants 5-year EU residency with no stay requirement — and you keep a tangible asset.

While Portugal and Spain have closed or gutted their real-estate routes, Greece kept its property-based Golden Visa alive — making it the go-to option for Americans who want an EU residency and a tangible asset rather than a fund subscription. But the rules tightened sharply in 2024–2025, and the entry price now depends heavily on where you buy. Here's the 2026 reality.

What is the Greece Golden Visa?

The Greece Golden Visa is a residency-by-investment program. A qualifying real-estate purchase earns you and your family a renewable 5-year residence permit, the right to live in Greece, and visa-free travel across the Schengen Area. There is no obligation to relocate — the permit has no minimum-stay requirement. You can rent the property out for income while holding it.

2026 investment thresholds

This is the part most outdated guides get wrong. The single €250,000 entry point is largely gone; thresholds are now tiered by location and property type:

Minimum investmentWhere it applies
€800,000Athens & Thessaloniki metro areas, Mykonos, Santorini, and islands with population over ~3,100
€400,000All other regions of Greece
€250,000Only properties bought for restoration, or commercial-to-residential conversion

A key structural rule: the investment must be in a single property meeting the threshold — you cannot stack several smaller units to reach it. For the €400,000 and €800,000 tiers, the property must also have a minimum habitable area of 120 square metres (interior floor space, excluding parking and storage). The €250,000 restoration/conversion route is not subject to the 120 sqm minimum. Additionally, €250K/€400K-tier properties must remain in qualifying use. On top of the purchase price, budget transfer tax, notary and legal fees, and per-family-member application costs.

Why Americans choose Greece

  • You keep a real asset: unlike fund-based visas, you own property you can use, rent, or later sell.
  • No stay requirement: hold the permit while continuing to live in the US.
  • Whole family included: spouse, dependent children, and dependent parents on one investment.
  • Schengen access and an EU base, with a lower entry price than most Western-European programs (outside the €800K zones).

Residency, stay rules & the citizenship reality

The permit is valid for 5 years and renews as long as you keep the qualifying investment. Crucially, there is no minimum-stay requirement to maintain residency — ideal for a "plan B."

Citizenship is a different and much longer story. The Golden Visa does not grant Greek citizenship. To naturalize you must become a genuine resident — living in Greece at least 183 days per year for 7 years — and meet language and integration requirements. For most American investors, Greece is a residency and Schengen-access play, not a fast passport.

US & Greek tax impact

Holding the Golden Visa does not make you a Greek tax resident. You become tax resident only if you spend more than 183 days a year in Greece or make it your main home — which the program never requires.

As a US citizen you remain taxed on worldwide income. The practical points: rental income from the Greek property is reportable on your US return (Schedule E), a future sale is a US-reportable capital gain, and a Greek bank account can trigger FBAR/FATCA reporting. One advantage versus fund-based visas: direct real estate avoids the messy US PFIC rules that can apply to foreign investment funds — a nuance worth weighing if you're choosing between Greece and a Portugal-style fund route.

Step-by-step: applying as an American

  1. Get a Greek tax number (AFM) and open a Greek bank account.
  2. Identify the right zone & threshold for your target property (€250K/€400K/€800K).
  3. Engage an independent Greek lawyer and run title/zoning due diligence.
  4. Purchase the property via a notarial deed and register it.
  5. Submit the Golden Visa application with proof of funds, criminal-record certificate, and health insurance.
  6. Provide biometrics and receive the 5-year residence permits for you and your family.
  7. Plan your US tax compliance (rental reporting, FBAR) before year-end.

Is the Greece Golden Visa worth it in 2026?

For Americans who want EU optionality plus a usable Mediterranean asset, Greece remains one of the strongest options — especially in the €400K regions away from the premium zones. Just go in with current thresholds, a single qualifying property, and clean US tax planning. The "€250K beach apartment in Santorini" era is over; the smart 2026 play is a well-chosen property in a €400K region or a genuine restoration project.

Frequently asked questions

How much do you need for the Greece Golden Visa in 2026?

Tiered: €800,000 in Athens, Thessaloniki, Mykonos, Santorini and larger islands; €400,000 in most other regions; €250,000 only for restoration or commercial-to-residential conversions.

Can Americans get the Greece Golden Visa?

Yes. US citizens are non-EU nationals and fully eligible. You qualify via a property investment meeting the zone minimum, plus proof of funds, a criminal-record certificate, and health insurance.

Is there a stay requirement?

No. The 5-year permit has no minimum-stay requirement. Qualifying for citizenship later requires 183+ days per year for 7 years.

Does it lead to citizenship?

Not directly. Citizenship requires becoming a genuine resident (183+ days/year for 7 years) and meeting naturalization rules including language.

Can I include my family?

Yes. One qualifying investment can include your spouse, dependent children, and dependent parents.
