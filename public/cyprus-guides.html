Cyprus Real Estate & Residency Guides

Lawyer-reviewed guides for Americans buying property in Cyprus — the legal and tax reality, not the brochure.

Cyprus is an English-speaking, common-law EU island where a single new-build purchase can secure lifetime residency for your whole family. It has no annual property tax, and as of 2026 no stamp duty on a purchase. The two things that decide your outcome: which side of the VAT-vs-transfer-fee fork you buy on, and getting your US tax layer right. The guides below go deep on each; this page is the map.

Why Americans look at Cyprus

For a US buyer, Cyprus solves problems that most Mediterranean markets create. It runs on English common law, so a contract of sale reads the way an American expects and the courts operate in English. It is inside the European Union, giving you a Schengen-adjacent base and EU-grade banking and healthcare. And its property-holding costs are among the lowest in Europe: the national annual property tax was abolished in 2017, and stamp duty on purchase contracts ended on 1 January 2026. Layer on a permanent-residency permit that never expires for a €300,000 purchase, and you have a rare combination of legal familiarity, EU access, and low friction.

The catch is that the simplicity is only skin-deep. Cyprus taxes a purchase under one of two mutually exclusive regimes — VAT on new builds or transfer fees on resales — and choosing the wrong one can swing your total cost by tens of thousands of euros on the same property. Non-EU buyers (Americans included) also need an acquisition permit, and there is a US tax layer that follows the property home. Those are the details our guides exist to get right.

Where to buy — the regions

Cyprus is small, but its districts behave like different markets, each with its own buyer:

  • Limassol — the business and finance capital, home to the marina and the highest prices on the island. This is where corporate relocations, funds and the priciest new-build towers concentrate. Strong rental demand, but you pay for it.
  • Paphos — the long-standing favourite for American and British retirees and remote workers. The most established expat community, gentler prices than Limassol, an international airport, and a large stock of villas and residency-qualifying new builds.
  • Larnaca — the value play. The main airport, a regenerating seafront, and lower entry prices make it the district many buyers now watch for appreciation.
  • Nicosia — the inland capital. Less holiday appeal, but a real year-round city with local rental demand and yields that coastal resort towns rarely match.
  • Famagusta district (Ayia Napa / Protaras) — resort and holiday-let territory, strong in summer season, quieter off-peak. Best suited to buyers focused on short-term rental income.

One boundary matters legally: everything here refers to the Republic of Cyprus. Property in the Turkish-occupied north sits under a separate, internationally unrecognised title system and carries serious ownership risk — our guides deliberately do not cover it.

Model the total cost both ways before you fall in love with a property.

Who Cyprus suits — and who should look elsewhere

Cyprus fits retirees who want an English-speaking EU base with a lifetime permit and near-zero holding costs; remote entrepreneurs who want EU footing without a language barrier; and investors who value a low-tax exit (20% CGT with generous exemptions, no annual tax) over maximum rental yield.

Cyprus is a weaker fit if you need a fast route to an EU passport — this is residency, not citizenship, and the old citizenship-by-investment scheme is gone. It is also less compelling if you are chasing the highest possible rental yields, which inland and larger EU cities often beat, or if your budget sits below the level where the VAT math and permit costs make sense. Cyprus FAQ

Is Cyprus a good place for Americans to buy property in 2026?

For an English-speaking, common-law EU base with a lifetime residency path and very low holding costs, it is one of the strongest options in the Mediterranean. The main risks are specific rather than systemic: getting the VAT-versus-transfer-fee math wrong, skipping the specific-performance lodgement, or buying a unit with a hidden developer mortgage.

Do Americans need residency or a visa to buy in Cyprus?

No — you do not need to be a resident to buy. As a non-EU national you need a Council of Ministers acquisition permit to register the title, but that is separate from residency. Buying can, however, open the door to residency if you invest €300,000 or more in new property.

Which part of Cyprus is best for a first purchase?

It depends on the goal: Paphos for retirees and the largest expat community, Limassol for prestige and business, Larnaca for value and appreciation, Nicosia for year-round rental yield. All sit in the Republic of Cyprus — avoid the Turkish-occupied north entirely.

How long does it take to buy?

A clean resale typically completes in about four to eight weeks. The acquisition permit and any residency application then run in parallel over the following months.

Will I owe US tax on a Cyprus property?

Possibly. The US taxes worldwide income, so Cypriot rent and a future sale are reportable, though the foreign tax credit and the US–Cyprus treaty relieve most double tax. The property itself is not FBAR/Form 8938 reportable, but the Cyprus bank account you open to buy it can be.
